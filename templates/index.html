Project Aim

This project aims to demonstrate the principles of computed tomography (CT) through the implementation of the Radon transform and its inverse.

CT scanning is a critical medical imaging technique that allows for non-invasive visualization of internal structures. By understanding how 2D images are transformed into sinograms (projections at different angles) and then reconstructed back into images, users can gain insight into the mathematical foundations of modern medical imaging.

Project Objectives
  • To demonstrate the mathematical principles behind CT scan reconstruction
  • To visualize the Radon transform and its inverse in action
  • To provide an interactive tool for understanding projection angles
  • To illustrate the importance of sufficient angular sampling in CT imaging
  • To show the practical application of image processing techniques in medical imaging
Procedure
  1. Select an image file from your device by clicking the "Choose Image" button in the Simulation tab.
  2. The image will be converted to grayscale for processing.
  3. The Radon transform will be applied to create a sinogram, which represents projections of the image at different angles.
  4. The inverse Radon transform will then reconstruct the original image from these projections.
  5. The results will display the original image, the sinogram, and the reconstructed image for comparison.
  6. You can observe how the reconstruction quality depends on the number of projection angles used.
